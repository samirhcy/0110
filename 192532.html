Some said it was a ghost, others claimed it was a creature from another realm, but no one had ever seen it and lived to tell the tale.

The townspeople lived in fear of this mysterious entity, and many avoided venturing into the woods at all costs. But one brave soul, a young woman named Eliza, was determined to uncover the truth. She had heard the whispers and seen the fear in her neighbors' eyes, and she couldn't rest until she knew what lurked in the darkness.

One moonlit night, Eliza ventured into the woods, armed with nothing but a lantern and her unyielding curiosity. As she made her way through the dense trees, she felt the weight of the unknown pressing in around her. The wind whispered through the branches, and the shadows seemed to dance with a life of their own.

Suddenly, she heard a soft, melodic hum floating through the air. It was a haunting tune that seemed to beckon her deeper into the heart of the woods. Unable to resist, Eliza followed the sound until she came upon a clearing bathed in moonlight.

The entity spoke to Eliza, weaving a tale of forgotten realms and lost dreams. It told her of riddles and mysteries, of secrets hidden in plain sight. And then, with a knowing smile, it posed a riddle to Eliza:

"The more you take, the more you leave behind. What am I?"
